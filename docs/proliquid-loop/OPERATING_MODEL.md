# Proliquid Loop Operating Model

## Purpose

This document defines how Proliquid should operate as a Luxembourg-focused accounting, reporting, and financial administration platform. It translates the strategic direction in `VISION.md` into a practical operating model for day-to-day service delivery, financial control, and client coordination.

The model assumes that clients come to Proliquid first for Luxembourg credibility and operational support, then remain because the platform provides stronger visibility, discipline, and continuity across administration and reporting.

## Actors

### Clients

Proliquid serves clients with real administrative and reporting needs around Luxembourg structures, including:

- Entrepreneurs establishing Luxembourg structures
- International investors
- Holding companies
- SPVs and private investment vehicles
- Family offices
- Real estate investment structures
- Cross-border groups

### Proliquid Service Teams

The core service model combines human expertise across:

- Company Formation
- Banking Assistance
- Accounting & Tax
- Holding & SPV Administration
- Investor Reporting
- Family Office Support

These teams are responsible for delivering the regulated, judgment-based, and relationship-driven parts of the operating model.

### Proliquid Platform

The platform supports service delivery through:

- Secure Client Portal
- Entity Dashboard
- Secure Document Room
- Investor Access
- Accounting Visibility
- Audit Trail
- Workflow Controls

The platform is not the service by itself. It is the control layer through which Proliquid organizes documents, financial data, review steps, deadlines, and client visibility.

## Core Business Objects

The operating model revolves around a small set of business objects that must remain clear, controlled, and traceable.

### Client And Structure Objects

- Client
- Organization
- Entity
- Holding structure
- SPV or investment vehicle
- Counterparty
- Investor or shareholder

These objects define who Proliquid serves, what legal structures exist, and how responsibilities are grouped.

### Financial And Administrative Objects

- Accounting periods
- Chart of accounts
- Accounting rules
- Business transactions
- Journal entries
- Documents
- Bank statements
- Reporting packs
- Compliance deadlines

These objects define the operational record of the structure and must be governed carefully.

### Control Objects

- User roles and access rights
- Review queues
- Statuses
- Audit logs
- Exceptions
- Escalations

These objects ensure that financial operations remain supervised, explainable, and fit for institutional use.

## Operational Workflows

### 1. Understand the structure

The first phase is diagnostic. Proliquid must understand the legal form, ownership, intended activity, reporting requirements, banking context, and stakeholder expectations of the Luxembourg structure.

Typical outputs:

- structure summary
- service scope
- onboarding requirements
- reporting expectations
- initial responsibility map

### 2. Set up the Luxembourg vehicle

This phase covers company formation and structural setup. The objective is to establish the legal vehicle correctly and prepare the administrative base for future accounting and reporting.

Typical outputs:

- legal entity established
- constitutional and supporting documentation collected
- ownership and control records organized
- initial entity record created

### 3. Prepare banking and compliance

This phase coordinates banking assistance and administrative readiness. The objective is to prepare the structure to operate with complete, supportable records.

Typical outputs:

- bank account file prepared
- KYC and compliance documents organized
- key administrative dependencies tracked
- operating readiness confirmed

### 4. Run accounting and reporting

This is the main recurring operating loop. Proliquid receives financial source documents, records transactions, applies accounting controls, manages period discipline, and prepares internal or external reporting outputs.

Typical recurring activities:

- collect and organize documents
- review financial events
- prepare accounting entries
- validate supporting evidence
- manage periods and close cadence
- produce investor or management reporting

### 5. Monitor through Proliquid

The final phase is not an endpoint but a continuous control layer. Clients and Proliquid teams monitor the structure through a secure workspace that centralizes entity visibility, documents, status, reporting, and review history.

Typical outcomes:

- clearer operational visibility
- faster follow-up on missing items
- stronger continuity across reporting periods
- more disciplined investor communication

## Human Responsibilities

Human teams remain responsible for all activities that require judgment, accountability, legal context, or financial sign-off.

### Service Delivery Responsibilities

- understand the purpose and substance of the structure
- define service scope and reporting cadence
- coordinate company formation and administrative setup
- prepare banking support files and compliance follow-up
- maintain accounting records and tax support work
- manage holding, SPV, and family office administration
- prepare and review investor reporting outputs

### Financial Control Responsibilities

- validate the economic meaning of transactions
- review source documents for completeness and coherence
- approve accounting treatment where judgment is required
- ensure period discipline before posting
- review exceptions, anomalies, and escalations
- confirm the quality of final reporting

### Relationship Responsibilities

- communicate with clients, banks, auditors, tax advisers, and investors
- request missing documents or clarifications
- resolve ambiguities that software cannot settle safely
- provide institutional accountability for final outcomes

## AI Responsibilities

AI should support throughput, consistency, and early detection of issues, but it should not replace accountable financial judgment.

### Appropriate AI Responsibilities

- classify documents into likely categories
- extract candidate fields from invoices or statements
- suggest likely counterparties, accounts, or workflows
- detect missing information or unusual inconsistencies
- summarize operational status and outstanding tasks
- help prioritize review queues and follow-up actions

### AI Boundaries

AI should not:

- make unsupervised accounting decisions with legal or tax consequences
- post journal entries without approved governance
- override period controls
- bypass human review on ambiguous cases
- fabricate missing evidence or assumptions

## Levels of Automation

Automation should be graduated, not binary. Proliquid should apply the lightest level of automation that remains safe and operationally useful.

### Level 1: Structured visibility

The platform centralizes entities, documents, statuses, reporting items, and control signals. This is the baseline layer and should exist for every structure.

### Level 2: Assisted preparation

The platform can assist with document intake, classification, routing, extraction suggestions, and pre-filled accounting or reporting drafts. Human review remains explicit.

### Level 3: Controlled workflow automation

The platform can automate reminders, routing, approval steps, review sequencing, status transitions, and recurring operational tasks where rules are stable and low-risk.

### Level 4: Conditional accounting automation

Limited accounting automation may be allowed only where rules are explicit, supporting data is complete, and exceptions are routed back to humans. This level must remain tightly governed.

### Level 5: Autonomous financial action

This level is not part of the intended operating model for critical accounting decisions. Proliquid should remain a controlled financial operations environment, not an unsupervised autopilot.

## Governance Rules

The operating model must preserve professional discipline across all entities and service lines.

### General Rules

- every material financial action must be traceable
- every structure must have clear ownership of service responsibilities
- documents must support accounting outcomes
- period controls must be respected
- investor-facing reporting must come from organized source records

### Accounting Rules

- draft work may be assisted, but posting must remain controlled
- journal integrity is more important than speed
- exceptions must be visible rather than silently absorbed
- reporting outputs must align with approved accounting records

### Access And Information Rules

- clients should see what is relevant to them and no more
- internal teams should have access consistent with role and responsibility
- sensitive data should be governed through role-based access and auditability

## Escalation Principles

Escalation is a control function, not a failure.

### Escalate When

- source documentation is missing or contradictory
- the legal or tax context is unclear
- a proposed accounting treatment is ambiguous
- a deadline risk threatens reporting quality
- investor communication depends on unresolved financial data
- a workflow exception exceeds predefined tolerance

### Escalation Expectations

- surface the issue early
- preserve the full evidence trail
- assign a clear owner
- separate fact gathering from decision making
- resolve the issue at the right level of seniority

Escalations should protect quality, not slow the platform unnecessarily.

## Future Operating Model

Over time, Proliquid should move from a service operation supported by software to a highly structured operating loop where service, control, and visibility are tightly integrated.

### Near-Term Direction

- stronger document intake discipline
- cleaner accounting preparation workflow
- better reporting continuity across periods
- improved visibility for clients and internal teams

### Medium-Term Direction

- AI-assisted extraction and classification
- controlled pre-accounting drafts
- more structured review queues
- better linkage between documents, accounting, and reporting

### Long-Term Direction

The future operating model should allow Proliquid to serve more Luxembourg structures with stronger consistency, faster cycle times, and better institutional control, without weakening human accountability in accounting, reporting, or administration.

## Notes

This operating model should be used to guide future product decisions, workflow design, and service policy. It should remain practical, conservative in financial control, and aligned with the realities of Luxembourg structure administration.
