# Proliquid Onboarding Loop

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the minimum viable client onboarding loop for Proliquid. It describes how a new client request should move from first contact to a live entity workspace that is ready for controlled accounting, document intake, reporting, and operational follow-up.

The onboarding loop exists to ensure that:

- new mandates are qualified before operational work begins
- entity information is collected in a structured way
- compliance, banking, accounting, and portal requirements are identified early
- the client receives a live workspace only when the minimum operating foundation is in place
- internal teams start from a controlled and consistent operating record

This loop should be treated as the first launch-critical operating loop because the rest of the platform depends on onboarding quality.

## 2. Scope

This onboarding loop applies to:

- new Luxembourg holding company mandates
- new SPV and private investment vehicle mandates
- new family office-related structures
- existing Luxembourg entities being brought into Proliquid operations
- multi-entity client relationships where each entity still requires its own operating record

The loop covers onboarding through workspace readiness. It does not cover the full recurring operating lifecycle after the entity goes live.

## 3. Target Client Profiles

The onboarding model is designed for the main Proliquid client groups already established in the documentation.

### Primary target profiles

- entrepreneurs establishing Luxembourg structures
- international investors
- holding companies
- SPVs and private investment vehicles
- family offices
- real estate investment structures
- cross-border groups

### Onboarding implication

The loop should assume that different profiles require different depth of information, but the same control posture:

- clear structure identification
- clear ownership context
- clear service scope
- clear operating readiness before activation

## 4. Onboarding Entry Points

The loop should accept a limited number of clean entry points so that onboarding starts with enough context.

### Entry points

- direct client mandate request
- referral from adviser, fiduciary, lawyer, or banking contact
- existing client requesting onboarding of a new entity
- family office or group client requesting onboarding of multiple related structures
- internal conversion from a prior non-platform service relationship

### Entry-point rule

No onboarding should begin as a fully open-ended intake. Each entry point should identify:

- who the client is
- what structure is involved or intended
- what service outcome is being requested
- who the initial internal owner is

## 5. Required Client Information

The onboarding loop should collect the minimum client-level information required to evaluate fit, ownership, and service scope.

### Minimum required client information

- client name
- main contact person
- contact details
- country or countries of relevance
- client type or profile
- reason for using Luxembourg
- expected service scope

### Ownership and relationship context

- ultimate beneficial ownership context
- principal decision-makers
- investor or shareholder context where relevant
- relationship to existing Proliquid entities if applicable

### Client information rule

If the client objective, ownership context, or expected service scope is unclear, onboarding should not advance to workspace creation without human clarification.

## 6. Required Entity Information

Each entity must be defined as an operating unit before Proliquid can manage it properly.

### Minimum required entity information

- entity name
- legal form
- jurisdiction
- incorporation status
- expected activity or purpose
- base currency
- ownership structure summary
- directors or authorized representatives

### Additional entity context where relevant

- whether the entity is a holding, SPV, family office vehicle, or operating investment structure
- whether the entity is new or already active
- expected reporting cadence
- key stakeholders who need portal visibility

### Entity information rule

An entity should not go live in the platform without enough structural clarity to determine:

- what it is
- why it exists
- who controls it
- what Proliquid is expected to do for it

## 7. Required Compliance And Banking Information

Compliance and banking readiness are part of onboarding, not a downstream administrative afterthought.

### Minimum compliance information

- core identification documents
- ownership and control documentation
- KYC-related inputs required for service acceptance
- key jurisdictional sensitivities if known

### Minimum banking information

- whether a bank account already exists
- bank name and account context if existing
- whether banking assistance is required
- expected banking flows or operating pattern at a high level

### Compliance and banking rule

If there are material unknowns around ownership, control, or banking readiness, the entity may be onboarded only into a controlled pending state rather than treated as fully operational.

## 8. Required Accounting Setup Information

The onboarding loop must gather enough accounting information to create an initial operating foundation.

### Minimum accounting setup information

- accounting standard or operating basis to be applied
- first reporting period context
- expected transaction profile
- expected document types
- known key counterparties where available
- whether the structure will have invoices, bank statements, investor flows, intercompany balances, or distributions

### Additional accounting context

- whether historical data migration is required
- whether opening balances exist
- whether there are existing unresolved reconciliations or legacy accounting issues

### Accounting setup rule

The entity workspace may go live before full accounting production starts, but accounting operations should not be treated as ready until the minimum accounting setup is reviewed and understood.

## 9. Required Portal Access Information

Portal access should be defined during onboarding so the workspace is role-appropriate from day one.

### Minimum access information

- internal Proliquid owner
- internal accountant or reviewer roles if known
- client users who require access
- investor or family office viewers if applicable
- role intention for each user

### Access-definition rule

No user should receive portal access without a clear reason tied to:

- operational responsibility
- reporting visibility
- document access need
- investor or stakeholder mandate

## 10. Onboarding Workflow

The onboarding loop should follow a structured sequence that prioritizes qualification and operating readiness.

### Workflow

Client Request Received  
Initial Qualification  
Entity or Structure Information Collected  
Compliance And Banking Information Collected  
Accounting Setup Information Collected  
Portal Roles Defined  
Human Review  
Entity Workspace Created  
Initial Operating Checklist Generated  
Onboarding Completed

### Step 1: Client Request Received

The mandate or request enters Proliquid through an approved entry point and is assigned an internal owner.

### Step 2: Initial Qualification

Proliquid confirms that:

- the structure type fits the target market
- the requested service scope is understood at a basic level
- the client is a plausible operational fit

### Step 3: Entity Or Structure Information Collected

The entity record is defined with enough legal, purpose, and ownership clarity to support future operations.

### Step 4: Compliance And Banking Information Collected

Core ownership, KYC, and banking readiness inputs are collected or clearly marked as pending.

### Step 5: Accounting Setup Information Collected

The accounting and reporting operating profile is defined sufficiently for a controlled start.

### Step 6: Portal Roles Defined

Internal and external access intentions are documented before the workspace is activated.

### Step 7: Human Review

A designated internal reviewer confirms that the onboarding record is coherent and sufficient for a live workspace.

### Step 8: Entity Workspace Created

The entity is created as a live operating unit with its initial structure, access, and status context.

### Step 9: Initial Operating Checklist Generated

The client and internal team receive the first actionable list of what remains to begin normal operations.

### Step 10: Onboarding Completed

The entity is considered onboarded when the workspace is live, the internal owner is clear, and the first operational next steps are explicitly visible.

## 11. Human Review Points

Human review is required because onboarding determines the integrity of everything that follows.

### Mandatory human review points

- after initial qualification
- after structure and ownership information collection
- before the entity workspace is made live
- before sensitive external users receive access

### Reviewers should confirm

- the client and entity fit the target operating model
- the service scope is clear enough to proceed
- the ownership and control picture is coherent
- compliance and banking dependencies are understood
- accounting readiness is realistic
- portal access is appropriately scoped

## 12. AI Assistance Opportunities

AI may support onboarding preparation, but it should not replace human judgment on fit, risk, or structural interpretation.

### Appropriate AI assistance

- summarize intake information
- identify missing fields or inconsistencies
- suggest likely structure profile
- organize onboarding inputs into a cleaner record
- propose an initial operating checklist
- highlight likely reporting or banking dependencies

### AI boundaries

AI should not:

- approve onboarding
- determine compliance sufficiency by itself
- decide access rights autonomously
- infer ownership or legal meaning without human review

## 13. Escalation Cases

Escalation is required when onboarding cannot be completed safely through the normal flow.

### Escalation triggers

- unclear beneficial ownership or control
- unclear legal structure or intended activity
- material compliance uncertainty
- banking readiness issue that affects operational viability
- disagreement on service scope
- unusual investor or shareholder access requirements
- uncertainty over whether the entity can enter live operations

### Escalation rule

Where an onboarding question affects legal, compliance, accounting, or access risk materially, the item must be escalated before the entity is treated as fully live.

## 14. Output Of A Completed Onboarding

A completed onboarding should produce a live, usable, and controlled starting point.

### Minimum outputs

- validated client record
- validated entity record
- defined initial service scope
- documented compliance and banking status
- documented accounting setup baseline
- defined portal roles
- live entity workspace
- initial operating checklist
- named internal owner

### Good completed-onboarding outcome

At the end of onboarding, the client should know what happens next, and the internal team should not need to reconstruct the mandate from scratch.

## 15. Loop Success Criteria

The onboarding loop is successful when it creates a clean operational handoff into the live Proliquid platform.

### Operational success criteria

- onboarding requests are qualified quickly
- required information is collected in a structured way
- ownership, scope, and operating purpose are clear
- the workspace goes live without major internal ambiguity

### Control success criteria

- compliance and banking unknowns are visible
- access rights are defined intentionally
- accounting readiness is not overstated
- escalations are surfaced before go-live, not after

### Client success criteria

- the client experiences an orderly onboarding journey
- the client receives a live workspace with clear next steps
- the client understands what Proliquid needs and what Proliquid will do

## Notes

This onboarding loop should remain practical, launch-oriented, and conservative in its control posture. It is intended to turn new mandates into live, usable entity workspaces without sacrificing structural clarity or operational discipline.
