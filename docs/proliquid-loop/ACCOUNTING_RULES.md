# Proliquid Loop Accounting Rules

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the foundational accounting policy that future Proliquid loops must follow when preparing, classifying, reviewing, and controlling accounting activity.

It is designed for Luxembourg holding companies, SPVs, family offices, and investment structures. Its purpose is not to reproduce Lux GAAP in full. Its purpose is to provide a practical operating rulebook that allows Proliquid teams and future AI-assisted loops to handle recurring accounting decisions consistently, conservatively, and with proper human control.

This document should be used whenever Proliquid must decide how to:

- classify financial events
- map documents to accounting outcomes
- assess confidence
- decide when human review is mandatory
- manage exceptions and escalation

## 2. Accounting Philosophy

Proliquid should follow a control-first accounting philosophy.

### Core principles

- substance matters more than label
- supporting evidence matters more than speed
- consistency matters more than cleverness
- traceability matters more than automation volume
- conservative classification is preferred when evidence is incomplete

### Operating posture

For Luxembourg structures, many transactions are low-volume but high-consequence. A small number of transactions may drive legal, tax, investor, or governance outcomes. For that reason:

- material ambiguity must not be hidden
- accounting must remain document-supported
- exceptional items must be surfaced early
- the system may assist classification, but it must not replace accountable review where judgment is needed

## 3. Human Validation Principles

Human validation is a mandatory control layer.

### Human validation is always required when:

- the transaction is ambiguous
- the source document is incomplete, contradictory, or missing
- VAT treatment is uncertain
- the counterparty cannot be identified confidently
- the economic purpose of the payment is unclear
- the transaction is unusual for the structure
- the value is high relative to the normal activity of the entity
- the entry affects loans, equity, distributions, acquisitions, disposals, or related-party balances
- the transaction may affect investor reporting materially

### Human validation remains mandatory for high-value transactions

Each entity should have a value threshold defined operationally by Proliquid based on its size, transaction profile, and reporting sensitivity. Until entity-specific thresholds are defined, any transaction that is large relative to the entity's normal recurring operating pattern must be treated as high-value and reviewed by a human before final accounting acceptance.

### Human reviewers are responsible for:

- confirming economic substance
- confirming account selection
- confirming VAT treatment
- confirming period allocation
- confirming whether the transaction is ordinary, exceptional, or governance-sensitive

## 4. Chart of Accounts Structure

The chart of accounts must support clarity, not only statutory compliance.

### Structural principles

- balance sheet and profit and loss categories must remain clearly separated
- client entities must use a structured chart that supports recurring classification
- custom accounts may be added only when they improve clarity without weakening consistency
- accounts should be specific enough to support reporting, but not so fragmented that consistency is lost

### Practical structure for Proliquid loops

The operating model should distinguish clearly between:

- equity and capital accounts
- shareholder and investor balances
- loans and intercompany balances
- bank and cash accounts
- receivables and payables
- operating expenses
- professional fees and administration costs
- taxes and VAT accounts
- finance income and finance expense
- investment income and distributions
- exceptional or non-recurring items

### Account selection rule

When choosing between a broad account and a more specific account, the more specific account should be used only if:

- the supporting evidence is clear
- the classification can be applied consistently in future periods
- the distinction improves reporting usefulness

If those conditions are not met, use the more stable standard account and flag the item for possible refinement later.

## 5. Expense Classification Rules

Expenses must be classified according to economic purpose, not only vendor name.

### Standard operating expense categories

Recurring expenses should normally be classified into practical categories such as:

- professional fees
- legal fees
- tax advisory fees
- audit fees
- accounting and administration fees
- banking fees
- office and operating support
- software and data subscriptions
- regulatory or filing costs
- travel or meeting costs if relevant to the entity

### Expense classification rules

- classify based on what was purchased or provided
- do not classify based only on historic habit if the current invoice differs
- separate professional service categories where reporting value is meaningful
- keep immaterial items in stable recurring categories rather than creating unnecessary fragmentation

### Expense review triggers

Human review is required when:

- the invoice contains multiple materially different services
- the charge appears capital in nature rather than expense
- the supplier is related to the entity or structure
- the expense appears personal, unusual, or outside the entity's purpose
- the same supplier is used inconsistently across periods

## 6. Revenue Classification Rules

Revenue must be classified based on the nature of the inflow and the role of the entity.

### Common revenue or inflow types for target structures

Depending on the structure, relevant categories may include:

- service income
- management fee income
- dividend income
- interest income
- rental or asset income
- gain-related or investment-related proceeds
- reimbursement income

### Revenue classification rules

- distinguish operating revenue from investment income
- distinguish recurring revenue from one-off inflows
- distinguish true revenue from capital injections, loans, or reimbursements
- do not classify a bank receipt as revenue unless supporting evidence confirms that it is earned income

### Mandatory review cases

Human validation is required when:

- the entity is primarily a holding or SPV with limited ordinary trading activity
- the inflow could be a loan, capital contribution, shareholder funding, or reimbursement
- the source of funds is not clearly documented
- the receipt is large or unusual

## 7. Bank Transaction Classification Rules

Bank transactions should never be treated as self-explanatory. The bank line is evidence of movement, not full evidence of accounting substance.

### Initial bank classification hierarchy

When a bank movement is received, Proliquid should first determine whether it is likely:

- incoming or outgoing
- operational, financing, investing, tax-related, or internal
- linked to an invoice, statement support, contract, or known recurring pattern

### Default bank transaction rules

- a bank reference alone is not enough for final classification where ambiguity exists
- recurring known charges may be proposed automatically if prior treatment is stable
- shareholder, intercompany, and investment-related movements must be reviewed carefully
- unexplained incoming funds must not be assumed to be revenue
- unexplained outgoing funds must not be assumed to be expense

### High-risk bank categories

These categories require stronger review:

- shareholder transfers
- intercompany transfers
- loan drawdowns or repayments
- distributions
- capital injections
- tax payments with unclear basis
- large cash movements
- transfers between entity-owned accounts where purpose is unclear

## 8. Invoice Processing Rules

Invoices are operational source documents and must be processed with evidence discipline.

### Supplier invoice rules

- confirm supplier identity
- confirm invoice date
- confirm currency
- confirm amount and tax breakdown
- confirm service or goods description
- confirm whether the invoice belongs to the entity
- confirm whether the invoice is ordinary operating expenditure, investment-related, or exceptional

### Customer invoice rules

- confirm the nature of the billed service or income item
- confirm the commercial basis for the invoice
- confirm tax treatment
- confirm period allocation

### Invoice-to-accounting rules

- one invoice may map to one or more accounting lines if substance requires it
- mixed invoices should be split only where the split is material and well-supported
- if the supporting detail is weak, keep the accounting treatment conservative and request clarification

### Invoice review triggers

Human validation is required when:

- the invoice is materially large
- the supplier or customer is related-party
- the invoice description is vague
- the VAT position is unclear
- the invoice may relate to an acquisition, financing, restructuring, or distribution event

## 9. VAT Treatment Principles

VAT should be handled conservatively and only with clear supporting logic.

### General VAT principles

- VAT treatment must follow document-supported facts
- VAT must not be inferred casually from total amount alone
- absence of VAT on an invoice does not automatically mean exemption is correct
- VAT logic must consider the nature of the service, supplier location, client location, and entity profile

### Practical operating rules

- where the invoice clearly states VAT and the treatment is standard for the entity, VAT may be proposed
- where the invoice suggests reverse charge, exemption, or cross-border complexity, human review should confirm treatment
- where the entity's recoverability position is uncertain, the VAT treatment must be validated by a human

### Mandatory VAT review cases

- cross-border services
- mixed taxable and exempt structures
- family office or investment-related structures with uncertain recoverability
- unusual tax wording
- manual overrides

## 10. Reconciliation Rules

Reconciliation ensures that accounting records remain tied to real evidence.

### Core reconciliation expectations

- bank balances must reconcile to bank records
- invoices should reconcile to payments or open balances where relevant
- intercompany and shareholder balances must reconcile to support schedules
- reporting outputs must reconcile to approved accounting data

### Matching rules

- a match may be proposed only where the amount, timing, counterparty, and business context are reasonably aligned
- exact match is strongest, but near match may be acceptable if clearly explainable
- partial settlement should remain visible rather than collapsed into a misleading full match
- one-to-many or many-to-one matches require extra care and clear support

### Reconciliation review triggers

- unmatched material items
- old open balances
- unclear transfers
- repeated suspense-like patterns
- differences that affect investor, lender, or statutory reporting

## 11. Confidence Scoring Framework

Confidence scoring should help prioritize review. It should not be treated as a substitute for approval.

### High confidence

A transaction may be treated as high confidence when:

- the document is complete
- the counterparty is known
- the pattern is recurring
- the amount and description are coherent
- the account mapping is stable
- the VAT treatment is standard
- no governance-sensitive element is present

### Medium confidence

A transaction is medium confidence when:

- most evidence is present, but one important element remains inferred
- the pattern is plausible but not yet well-established
- account selection is likely but not definitive
- the item is ordinary but not fully routine

Medium-confidence items may be prepared automatically, but they should remain visible for review before final acceptance.

### Low confidence

A transaction is low confidence when:

- the source document is weak
- the counterparty is uncertain
- the transaction may be funding, loan, capital, or distribution related
- multiple accounting outcomes are plausible
- the VAT position is unclear
- the transaction is unusual or materially large

Low-confidence items must be escalated for explicit human validation.

## 12. Escalation Rules

Escalation is mandatory when risk exceeds routine operational handling.

### Escalate immediately when:

- legal or tax characterization is unclear
- a transaction may affect capital, debt, equity, or distributions
- an item may be misclassified as revenue or expense
- related-party activity is involved
- a material exception impacts reporting reliability
- a reviewer cannot explain the accounting outcome confidently

### Escalation destination

Escalation should go to the appropriate human owner based on the nature of the issue:

- accounting lead
- tax lead
- client manager
- senior reviewer
- external adviser where required

## 13. Exception Handling

Exceptions must be visible, categorized, and resolved explicitly.

### Common exception types

- missing document
- unclear counterparty
- ambiguous purpose
- VAT uncertainty
- period uncertainty
- duplicate or inconsistent record
- unsupported manual override

### Exception handling rules

- do not bury exceptions inside routine processing
- assign each exception an owner
- preserve the evidence trail
- keep provisional treatment separate from approved treatment where needed
- resolve material exceptions before final reporting sign-off

If temporary booking is necessary for operational continuity, it should be clearly identifiable and queued for review.

## 14. Audit Trail Requirements

Every meaningful accounting action should be reconstructable.

### Audit trail must capture

- source document reference
- who reviewed or approved the item
- classification outcome
- any override from the suggested treatment
- confidence level at the time of decision
- escalation status where relevant
- final accounting outcome

### Audit principles

- manual overrides must be visible
- approvals must be attributable
- changes in classification should be traceable over time
- the basis for high-risk decisions should be recoverable

The audit trail is not only a system record. It is part of Proliquid's control model and should support internal review, client explanation, auditor support, and future loop improvement.

## Notes

This document should remain practical, conservative, and operational. It is intended to support consistent decision-making across future Proliquid loops without weakening human accountability for Luxembourg financial structures.
